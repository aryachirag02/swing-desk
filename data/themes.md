FORWARD THEMES (weekly scan, 2026-07-17) — research, not signals

I'll search for upcoming catalysts for Indian equities focusing on government spending, PLI schemes, and sector cycles. Based on current tender pipelines and government capex cycles, here are the most concrete forward catalysts for Indian equities over the next 1-2 quarters:

BHARATNET PHASE-III FIBRE & EQUIPMENT TENDER (Q3-Q4 2026)
 BSNL initiated a Rs 650 billion tender process as part of the Rs 1.4 trillion revamped BharatNet project, aiming to enhance connectivity in rural areas.   The tender scope includes designing, supplying, constructing, and installing optical fibre cables, switches, routers, and other essential telecom equipment.  Direct exposure: Tejas Networks (fibre & optical equipment), HFCL (optical fibre), STL (telecom infrastructure). [Speculation: Nokia, Ericsson also bidding; local players face scale pressures.]

PLI ELECTRONICS & COMPONENT MANUFACTURING RAMP-UP (H2 2026)
 The Electronics Component Manufacturing Scheme (ECMS) is the largest and most commercially active PLI window, with 22 proposals approved by January 2026 with projected investments of Rs 41,863 crore and production of Rs 2.58 lakh crore.  Direct exposure: Kaynes Technology (PCB/components), Syrma SGS Technology (electronics contract manufacturing), ScanSource (distribution). [Speculation: Disbursements trail production by 4-6 months; timing dependent on FY25 verification cycles.]

PLI AUTOMOBILE & EV COMPONENTS ACCELERATION (Q3 2026 onward)
 Rs 1,999.94 crore has been released to five approved applicants for FY25 under the PLI Auto scheme.   The scheme has supported sales of 13,61,488 electric vehicle units including 10,42,172 e-2Ws and 2,38,385 e-3Ws.  Direct exposure: Sona BLW Precision Forgings (EV drivetrain components), Bharat Petroleum (EV infra concessions). [Speculation: e-2W/e-3W subsidy phase-out risk; component pricing under pressure.]

DEFENCE PROCUREMENT PIPELINE (Active through 2026-27)
 Over 4,720 defence tenders are currently active across Indian Air Force, Navy, and DRDO labs with closing dates spanning June-July 2026.  Direct exposure: Bharat Electronics (defence electronics), Bharat Dynamics (missiles), Garden Reach Shipbuilders (naval vessels). [Speculation: Order conversion timeline uncertain; capital-intensive with 12-18 month execution.]

PLI PHARMACEUTICALS & BULK DRUGS SCALE-UP (Q4 2026)
 PLI scheme sales for pharmaceuticals exceeded Rs 2.66 lakh crore over three years, including exports worth Rs 1.7 lakh crore.   Under the PLI Scheme for medical devices, 21 projects have started manufacturing 54 unique medical devices, including high-end devices such as LINAC, MRI, CT-Scan, Heart Valve, and Stent.  Direct exposure: Laurus Labs (bulk drugs PLI beneficiary), Narayana Hrudayalaya (medical device manufacturing). [Speculation: Import substitution gains already priced in for large pharma; smaller API players offer upside.]
